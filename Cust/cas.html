History of interactive media

Technically, a personal computer (PC) system with traditional magnetic-disk memory storage qualifies as a form of interactive media. More complex interactive systems have been in use since the mid-twentieth century, for example, as flight simulators in the aerospace industry. The term was popularized in the early 1990s, however, to describe PCs that incorporate high-capacity optical (laser) memory devices and digital sound systems.
